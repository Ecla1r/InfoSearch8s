<p><a data-api_path="/referents/7466327" href="https://genius.com/7466327" rel="noopener">True to his word,</a> Drake <a href="https://youtu.be/1yeOjly6qA0" rel="noopener nofollow">performed the song live at OVO Fest accompanied by fan-made memes that dissed Meek Mill</a>, a move taken <a data-api_path="/referents/8098585" href="https://genius.com/8098585" rel="noopener">straight from the Jay Z playbook.</a> This is widely considered as the point where Drake won the beef.</p>
<p>The song cover shows <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/c/cartejo01.shtml" rel="noopener nofollow">Joe Carter,</a> of Drake’s hometown team the Toronto Blue Jays, after he <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-F5HwiGm7lg" rel="noopener nofollow">hit the winning home run</a> from the 1993 World Series against Meek’s hometown team, the Philadelphia Phillies. The Blue Jays won the year before in 1992, earning “back to back” titles. <a href="https://twitter.com/JoeCarter_29/status/626458271495364608" rel="noopener nofollow">Carter went to twitter to personally thank Drake for using his picture as cover art.</a></p>
<p>The release date of this track was no mistake, as the Phillies and Blue Jays faced off that same night <a href="http://www.baseball-reference.com/boxes/TOR/TOR201507290.shtml" rel="noopener nofollow">(July 29th, 2015)</a> on Sportsnet. Poetically, the Blue Jays came out on top, 8-2.</p>
<p><a href="https://genius.com/discussions/174443-New-drake-back-to-back-freestyle" rel="noopener"><img alt="" data-animated="false" height="299" src="https://images.rapgenius.com/a7c27c4ecf1ad4f2e4d8a2c2434854c2.1000x299x1.jpg" width="1000"/></a></p>
<p>After Meek’s response track “Wanna Know” was released, Drake prepared a third diss track entitled <a href="http://mic.com/articles/123380/3peat-lyrics-here-s-the-song-drake-debuted-at-the-2015-ovo-festival" rel="noopener nofollow">“3Peat”, and played it at his OVOFest headlining show</a>. However, the diss was never released in full.</p>
<p>“Back To Back” was nominated in the <a href="http://uk.complex.com/music/2015/12/drake-back-to-back-first-grammy-nominated-diss-song" rel="noopener nofollow">“Best Rap Performance” category at the 2016 Grammy Awards, the first diss track to do so</a>.</p>
