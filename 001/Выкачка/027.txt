class="RichText__Container-oz284w-0 QmVDU"><p>“Formation” is a Black Power anthem, a fitting contribution to Black History Month and <a href="https://twitter.com/THEROYALKOURT/status/696081917116968960" rel="noopener nofollow">an important conversation piece</a> in the Black Lives Matter movement. The song was released one day after what would have been <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trayvon_Martin" rel="noopener nofollow">Trayvon Martin’s</a> 21st birthday and one day before what would have been <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_of_Sandra_Bland" rel="noopener nofollow">Sandra Bland’s</a> 29th birthday. Martin and Bland’s deaths are focal points of Black Lives Matter.</p>
<p>Continuing the trend commonly attributed to her <a data-api_path="/albums/65407" href="https://genius.com/albums/Beyonce/Beyonce" rel="noopener">surprise 2013 album release,</a> “Formation” was surprise-released alongside <a href="http://www.bustle.com/articles/140135-can-you-buy-formation-on-itunes-the-song-seems-to-be-very-exclusive-video" rel="noopener nofollow">an unlisted music video</a> the day before her appearance as part of the <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L_Hgh7sPDLM" rel="noopener nofollow">Super Bowl 50 halftime show,</a> where she performed this track. After her performance, she <a data-api_path="/songs/2419713" href="https://genius.com/Beyonce-the-formation-world-tour-lyrics" rel="noopener">announced The Formation World Tour.</a> “Formation” was later included as the closer for Beyoncé’s sixth album, <a data-api_path="/albums/150119" href="https://genius.com/albums/beyonce/lemonade" rel="noopener"><em>LEMONADE,</em></a> which was surprise-released on April 23, 2016.</p>
<p>New Orleans bounce musician <a data-api_path="/artists/148814" href="https://genius.com/artists/Big-freedia" rel="noopener">Big Freedia,</a> who appears on the interlude, noted the song and video showcase Bey <a href="http://www.fuse.tv/2016/02/beyonce-formation-big-freedia-new-song" rel="noopener nofollow">“paying homage to her roots.”</a> Bounce music originated in New Orleans and is a faster paced style of hip-hop music.</p>
<p>Shots from the music video come directly from Chris Black and Abteen Bagheri’s 2012 <em>That B.E.A.T.</em>, a bounce music documentary filmed in New Orleans. After <a href="http://www.ew.com/article/2016/02/06/beyonce-formation-music-video-that-beat" rel="noopener nofollow">some initial controversy,</a> a rep for Beyoncé confirmed proper credit and compensation had been attributed to the film makers.
