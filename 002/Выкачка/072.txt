class="RichText__Container-oz284w-0 fqCywa"><p>Arguably the most <a href="https://www.complex.com/music/2018/10/the-50-best-hip-hop-diss-songs/2pac-hit-em-up-1996" rel="noopener nofollow">memorable diss track</a> of the East Coast–West Coast rivalry,  2Pac and The Outlawz hit up <a data-api_path="/artists/22" href="https://genius.com/artists/The-notorious-big" rel="noopener">Biggie Smalls</a>, <a data-api_path="/artists/82" href="https://genius.com/artists/Diddy" rel="noopener">P. Diddy</a>, and <a data-api_path="/artists/251" href="https://genius.com/artists/Mobb-deep" rel="noopener">Mobb Deep</a>, with direct threats of violence.</p>
<p>2Pac’s feud with Biggie and <a data-api_path="/artists/648028" href="https://genius.com/artists/Bad-boy-entertainment" rel="noopener">Bad Boy</a> was rooted in the <a href="https://www.xxlmag.com/today-in-hip-hop-2pac-shot-at-quad-studios-in-new-york-city/" rel="noopener nofollow">1994 shooting at the Quad Recording Studios</a>. Shakur believed that Bad Boy Records were involved in his shooting because Biggie and Puff Daddy were at the studio the same night he was <a href="https://2paclegacy.net/tupacs-shooting-new-york-1994/" rel="noopener nofollow">robbed and shot</a> in the studio lobby. Months after the incident, Biggie released <a data-api_path="/songs/371" href="https://genius.com/The-notorious-big-who-shot-ya-lyrics" rel="noopener">“Who Shot Ya?”</a> while Tupac was incarcerated for <a href="https://www.xxlmag.com/tupac-shakur-rape-accuser-woman-recalls-1993-assault-hotel-room/" rel="noopener nofollow">sexual abuse allegations</a>. Shakur was aggravated by the song and saw it as an insult, as noted in a 1995 <a href="https://2paclegacy.net/tupac-interview-kevin-powell-for-vibe-magazine/" rel="noopener nofollow"><em>Vibe</em> interview</a>.</p>
<p>In a June 2017 <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VmC8JzLgsb0" rel="noopener nofollow">interview with DJ Vlad</a>, Outlawz rappers <a data-api_path="/artists/2256" href="https://genius.com/artists/Edi-mean" rel="noopener">E.D.I. Mean</a> and <a data-api_path="/artists/2257" href="https://genius.com/artists/Young-noble" rel="noopener">Young Noble</a> revealed that 2Pac planned this track while he was still in prison. He began recording in May 1996 at the Cam-Am studios in Los Angeles and enlisted the help of <a data-api_path="/artists/8976" href="https://genius.com/artists/Johnny-j" rel="noopener">Johnny J</a>, who had worked on several songs on both <a data-api_path="/albums/42" href="https://genius.com/albums/2pac/Me-against-the-world" rel="noopener"><em>Me Against the World</em></a> and <a data-api_path="/albums/38" href="https://genius.com/albums/2pac/All-eyez-on-me" rel="noopener"><em>All Eyez On Me</em></a>. On this track, Johnny sampled the bassline from <a data-api_path="/artists/380975" href="https://genius.com/artists/Dennis-edwards" rel="noopener">Dennis Edwards</a>’s 1984 song, <a data-api_path="/songs/1784353" href="https://genius.com/Dennis-edwards-dont-look-any-further-lyrics" rel="noopener">“Don’t Look Any Further,”</a> and ‘Pac purposefully interpolated two songs from The Notorious B.I.G.’s group, <a data-api_path="/artists/129" href="https://genius.com/artists/Junior-mafia" rel="noopener">Junior M.A.F.I.A.</a>: 1995’s <a data-api_path="/songs/2287" href="https://genius.com/Junior-mafia-players-anthem-lyrics" rel="noopener">“Player’s Anthem”</a> and <a data-api_path="/songs/145" href="https://genius.com/Junior-mafia-get-money-lyrics" rel="noopener">“Get Money.”</a></p>
<p>On June 3, 1996, Shakur recorded a <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f03QKmXZr0k" rel="noopener nofollow">music video</a> for the song with The Outlawz. The video was filmed inside a warehouse and featured satiric impersonations of Biggie, Puffy, and <a data-api_path="/artists/411" href="https://genius.com/artists/Lil-kim" rel="noopener">Lil' Kim</a>.</p>
<p>This song spawned controversy and exacerbated the East Coast–West Coast rivalry even further. Biggie never responded directly but he took jabs at ‘Pac on <a data-api_path="/songs/368" href="https://genius.com/The-notorious-big-long-kiss-goodnight-lyrics" rel="noopener">“Long Kiss Goodnight.”</a> Mobb Deep retaliated with their 1996 track, <a data-api_path="/songs/4664" href="https://genius.com/Mobb-deep-drop-a-gem-on-em-lyrics" rel="noopener">“Drop A Gem On ‘Em.”</a></p>
<p>2Pac extended his beef with the East Coast on tracks such as <a data-api_path="/songs/286" href="https://genius.com/2pac-bomb-first-my-second-reply-lyrics" rel="noopener">“Bomb First (My Second Reply)”</a> and <a data-api_path="/songs/1085" href="https://genius.com/2pac-against-all-odds-lyrics" rel="noopener">“Against All Odds”</a> off of his November 1996 posthumous album, <a data-api_path="/albums/37" href="https://genius.com/albums/2pac/The-don-killuminati-the-7-day-theory" rel="noopener"><em>The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory</em></a>.</p></div></div></div><div
