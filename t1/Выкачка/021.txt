class="RichText__Container-oz284w-0 jKsAxS"><p>“Lose Yourself” is the theme song from Eminem’s semi-biographical 2002 movie <em><a data-api_path="/albums/38300" href="https://genius.com/albums/Various-artists/Music-from-and-inspired-by-the-motion-picture-8-mile" rel="noopener">8 Mile</a></em>. Eminem is narrating the life of the film’s protagonist, Jimmy, up until the third verse, where Jimmy and Eminem’s journey converge.</p>
<p>Engineer Steven King told <em>Rolling Stone</em> <a href="https://www.vgls.vic.gov.au/client/en_AU/vgls/search/detailnonmodal/eds:%24002f%24002f1657660369%24002f0%24002fazh%24007c%24007c15303517/ada/147dfac4-7ad0-4579-8819-8e2ff29dc5c7.Ujulbmo6f5%24002bZEYQ7Cf3SOTl%24002fq7NA4Wfb31Uu3nxmpvs%24003d?qu=Richman%2C+Daniel%2C+1939-&amp;if=ee%09edsExpanderFacet%09fulltext&amp;d=eds%3A%2F%2F1657660369%2F0%2Fazh%7C%7C15303517%7E1657660369%7E225&amp;ic=true&amp;te=1657660369&amp;av=0&amp;ps=300" rel="noopener nofollow">in 2003</a> that Eminem “laid down all three verses in one take”, which Eminem has since <a data-api_path="/referents/5140245" href="https://genius.com/5140246" rel="noopener">shed doubt on</a>.</p>
<p>Eminem stated in his 2008 autobiography, <a href="https://www.scribd.com/doc/94728297/The-Way-I-Am" rel="noopener nofollow"><em>The Way I Am</em></a>, that he wrote “in-between shooting scenes [for <em>8 Mile</em>] and taking care of [his] kids” (pg 108-109.) Scans of the scrawled lyrics on A4 writing pad pages were also featured in the autobiography (pg 217-218.)</p>
<p>In 2014, the <a data-api_path="/songs/586290" href="https://genius.com/Eminem-lose-yourself-original-demo-version-lyrics" rel="noopener">demo version</a> appeared on Shady Records' <a data-api_path="/albums/114275" href="https://genius.com/albums/Shady-records/Shadyxv" rel="noopener"><em>SHADY XV</em></a> compilation album, which featured the same beat and tone but almost entirely different lyrics.</p>
<p>Commercially, “Lose Yourself” was hugely successful and reached #1 in 20 countries, including the US. It marked Eminem’s first US #1, and held the top position for 12 weeks, becoming the third-longest chart-topper from a movie soundtrack (behind <a data-api_path="/songs/329470" href="https://genius.com/Whitney-houston-i-will-always-love-you-lyrics" rel="noopener">“I Will Always Love You”</a> and <a data-api_path="/songs/51652" href="https://genius.com/Boyz-ii-men-end-of-the-road-lyrics" rel="noopener">“End Of The Road”</a>). As of 2022, it is <a href="https://www.riaa.com/gold-platinum/?tab_active=default-award&amp;ar=EMINEM&amp;ti=LOSE+YOURSELF" rel="noopener nofollow">13x Multi-Platinum</a> in the US.</p>
<p>The song was lauded critically, too. It won <a href="https://www.grammy.com/grammys/awards/46th-annual-grammy-awards" rel="noopener nofollow">two Grammys</a> (Best Male Rap Solo Performance &amp; Best Rap Song) and became the first rap song to win the Oscar for <a href="http://www.oscars.org/oscars/ceremonies/2003" rel="noopener nofollow">Best Original Song</a>. Eminem didn’t attend the ceremony as he <a href="https://youtu.be/qV4tG-3V3do?t=173" rel="noopener nofollow">didn’t think</a> he’d win, meaning he didn’t perform it, which is atypical for winners of the category. This was <a href="https://variety.com/2020/music/news/eminem-oscar-academy-awards-lose-yourself-exclusive-interview-1203500085/" rel="noopener nofollow">remedied</a> when he performed the song at the <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qvSx_1WVWiM" rel="noopener nofollow">2020 Oscars</a>.</p>
<p>The song has also had a huge cultural impact. Apple used the song for <a href="https://appleinsider.com/articles/05/10/13/apple_pulls_new_eminem_ad_spot" rel="noopener nofollow">two commercials</a> in the mid-‘00s to promote iTunes and the iPod, with Eminem featuring in the latter video. Chrysler used the instrumental and Eminem for their 2011 <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mYsFUFgOEmM" rel="noopener nofollow">“Born of Fire”</a> Super Bowl commercial. In 2021, Eminem opened up a restaurant in Detroit called “Mom’s Spaghetti”, which is a lyric that’s been meme’d extensively (even being given its <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SW-BU6keEUw"
